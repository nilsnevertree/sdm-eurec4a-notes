Summary

In order to get an idea of how the rain evaporation changes between clouds (intra-clouds), CLEO was initialized for some individual clouds.

In more detail, Cloud clusters were used.

Results

Below, you can find the properties of all the clusters identified.

  • The size of the circles represents their LWC.
  • Only clusters are shown which have
    • altitude between 700 and 1100m and
    • with a vertical extent of less than 50m.
  • For 10 cloud clusters with the largest LWC, the annotations mark their given ID to identify them .

For these clusters, the dropsonde measurmensta were chosen which are not further away then 100 km and were launched not more than 3h befor or after the ATR flew through the cloud cluster. 

dropsonde_distance:
    max_spatial_distance: 100 #km
    max_temporal_distance: 3 hours
identification_type: clusters
split_radius: 4.5e-05 #m

It can be seen, that the cloud 18, shows less raining out.
This is suprising on a first view, because for cloud cluster 18, there seem to be much more rain droplets measured. But for cloud cluster 301, the fitted distribution is much broader. Thus also much larger rain droplets of 1 0 3 10^{3} Âµm may exist. As their mass scales with r 3 r^3 , the make a hugh part of the rain falling out of the cloud.

This changes after some minutes, when these big droplets reach the bottom of the domain.

It should be though of, how the fitting can be optimized to handle this.
